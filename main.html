Trends of Technological Innovations and Societal Attitudes

XIXth CENTURY

c.1800-c.1900

  • Turn of the Century to Civil War (c.1800-c.1855)

    Individualism

    America symbolized a break from old world corporatist mercantilism. All men worked for himself and technology served man's sucess.

    Representative Innovations

    Technology improved in production capability, e.g. milling machines, combine harvesters, etc.

  • Civil War & Reconstruction (c.1855c.1870

    American Unity

    Following the Civil War America wanted homogeneity and unity. Technology served to make Americans equal with one another.

    Representative Innovations

    Technology improved in power. Most utilized steam power and capitalized on its gains (Corliss engine). Electricity catches on.

  • Gilded Age (1870-1900)

    System

    Synthesis of individualism under American unity into system, wherein individuals worked under a common framework. Technology served to faciliate intercommunication, efficiency, etc.

    Representative Innovations

    Technology improved in efficiency. The new source of electricity was improved (e.g. electrical telegraph, lightbulb, etc.)

XXth CENTURY

c.1890-c.2000

  • Progressive Era (c.1980-c.1910)

    Progressive Imperialism and Isolationism

    A pair of movements: Gilded Age innovations prompted Americans to focus on domestic utility and American nationalism. This led to a surge in imperialism and subsequent reactionary isolationism. Technology served America's interests.

    Representative Innovations

    Surge in transportation technology (cars, airplanes, etc.) Industry boomed with the rise of assembly lines. Increased homogeneity.

  • World Wars(c.1916-c.1960)

    American Identity

    The world wars and the cold war cememnted American identity as mature and established. Technology served to protect America. new technology of computers rise.

    Representative Innovations

    A slew of military technology (bazookas, atomic weapons, etc., etc.) Computer technology.

  • Modern Time (c.1960-c.2000)

    Uncertain/Digitization

    It is uncertain what societal trend characterizes modern time, but the previous time periods prove that whatever it is, it is heavily associated, influenced by, and influences technological trends.

    Representative Innovations

    Much like the Gilded Age, Modern time capitalizes on the new technology of computers begun in the last era.
